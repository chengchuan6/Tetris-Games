HOW TO PLAY TETRIS

Tetris is the ultimate block-stacking puzzle game that challenges your reflexes and strategic thinking. The goal is simple: arrange falling tetrominoes (geometric shapes composed of four square blocks) to create complete horizontal lines, which then disappear from the playfield.

As you progress, the blocks fall faster, increasing the difficulty and excitement. The game ends when the stack of blocks reaches the top of the playing field and no new tetrominoes can enter.

Controls:

  • ←/→ Move blocks left/right
  • ↓ Soft drop (accelerate)
  • ↑ Rotate block
  • SPACE Hard drop (instant placement)

THE LEGACY OF TETRIS

Created by Russian software engineer Alexey Pajitnov in 1984, Tetris has become one of the most recognized and influential video games of all time. Its simple yet addictive gameplay has transcended generations and platforms, from its original implementation on the Electronika 60 to modern consoles, computers, and mobile devices.

The name "Tetris" comes from the Greek prefix "tetra-" (meaning "four") and the creator's favorite sport, tennis. Each piece in the game, known as a tetromino, consists of four squares arranged in different configurations.

Tetris is not just entertaining but has been studied for its cognitive benefits, including improved spatial reasoning, decision-making skills, and even potential therapeutic applications for traumatic memories and PTSD.

STRATEGIES FOR SUCCESS

Beginner Tips

  • • Always try to build a flat surface for future pieces
  • • Leave space for the I-piece (the long straight tetromino) to clear four lines at once
  • • Don't rush your decisions, especially at lower levels
  • • Learn to recognize piece patterns as they appear
  • • Practice using the preview window to plan your next move

Advanced Techniques

  • • Master T-spins for bonus points and efficient clearing
  • • Practice efficient piece stacking using the "7-bag" randomizer system
  • • Learn wall kicks and rotation systems to place pieces in tight spots
  • • Keep a "well" on the side for the I-piece to maximize Tetris clears
  • • Develop muscle memory for quick decision-making at higher speeds
